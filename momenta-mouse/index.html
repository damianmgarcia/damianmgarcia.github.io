MomentaMouse

About

MomentaMouse enables mouse-users to use touch-style scrolling and flicking gestures to navigate websites.

Web developers interested in this functionality simply need to import the module to begin automatically or manually creating MomentaMouse scrollers.

To get started, hold down your mouse's primary button on this page, or on one of the scrollers below, and then move the mouse around. The page, or scroller, will follow the movement of the cursor. If you do a flicking gesture, it will result in a scroll with momentum in proportion to the speed of your flick.

Some Things to Try

  • Scrolling on Nested Scrollers: Handling a single MomentaMouse scroller is simple, but how do you handle scrolling when there are nested scrollers or when a scroller is under another element such as an anchor or a button? Let's find out. First, let's test nested scrollers: Scroll vertically on one of the horizontal carousels below. Notice that the "main" scroller takes control from the horizontal carousel when you scroll vertically. Second, let's test a scroller under an anchor: Click and hold the "Repository" link on the red horizontal-only scroller below, and then scroll vertically to see which scroller takes control. Next, try moving horizontally to see which scroller takes control. Try the same tests with the other scroller types (vertical-only, etc.) to see how they react.
  • Highlighting and Dragging: The touch-screen convention for text-highlighting is to perform a long-press on a word to highlight it, and then to drag selection handles, if necessary, to change the selection. This convention is not necessary with MomentaMouse because mouse-users are also keyboard-users. Instead, a "Quick Toggle Key," which is the Ctrl key, is held down to temporarily deactivate MomentaMouse. This allows you to use your mouse as you normally would to highlight, copy, or drag things like text, links, or images. MomentaMouse will reactivate once the Ctrl key is let go. Try it out.
  • Overscroll and Bouncing: Overscroll and bouncing effects are useful visual indicators that the end of a scroller has been reached. Let's test them: While at the top or bottom of this page, or at one of the borders of any of the scrollers below, try pulling away. MomentaMouse will create an overscroll effect to let you know you have reached the end. To see a bounce, perform flick gestures with your mouse until the momentum scroll impacts a border. Try hitting the borders with varying amounts of force to see how they react. Notice that the intensity of the bounce is in proportion to the force of impact.
